# Model - Amazon Leadership Principles Behavioral Mapping

## LP / competency structure
Map each story to a primary LP and one secondary LP to avoid one-dimensional answers.

## STAR story mapping logic
- Situation/Task: business context + constraint.
- Action: candidate-owned decisions with alternatives considered.
- Result: measurable impact + mechanism + learning.

## Evidence quality rules
- Use concrete numbers, scope, and time windows.
- Include tradeoff and why chosen path won.
- Show ownership beyond assigned task boundaries.

## Weak-answer failure modes
- Story describes team output but not candidate contribution.
- Result is vague (“it improved”) without metrics.
- LP label is forced after the fact, not evidenced by actions.

## Reusable behavioral prep algorithm
1. Build LP-story matrix.
2. Score each story on evidence density.
3. Redraft weak stories to include constraints/tradeoffs.
4. Run timed mock and capture failure tags.
5. Rehearse revised high-risk LP stories.

## Story bank operating requirement
- Maintain a 10-15 story bank mapped across high-frequency LPs.
- Every story must pass STAR quality bar with explicit **I** ownership language, not generic **we** framing.
- Include metrics, constraint context, tradeoff rationale, and follow-up depth for probing.

## LP prompt categories
- Ownership and bias-for-action
- Customer obsession and long-term judgment
- Conflict/disagreement and influence without authority
- Failure, recovery, and learning velocity
- Ambiguity framing and decision quality
