## Part 1: How to Create a Routine

### 1. Prioritize Sleep as a High-Performance State

- Treat sleep as a supercharged brain state essential for productivity.
    
- Reframe sleep as a high-performance enhancer.
    
- Master the skill of sleep to fuel ambition and flow.
    

### 2. Build Your Sleep Container and Consistent Timing

- Establish consistent bedtime and wake-up routines.
    
- Reverse-plan your day to protect sleep time.
    
- Create a soothing bedtime routine with relaxing activities.
    
- Optimize your sleep environment:
    
    - Deaf: silence or white noise.
        
    - Blind: darkness or sleep mask.
        
    - Cold: ideal temperature between 15–19°C (60–67°F).
        
    - Hungry: last meal 4 hours before sleep.
        
- Power Down Ritual: offload thoughts to reduce cognitive load.
    
- Adjust fluid intake and invest in quality bedding.
    

### 3. Proactively Prevent Insomnia (CBTI Protocol)

- Use bed only for sleep and sex.
    
- Get up if awake for more than 15–20 minutes.
    
- Restrict time in bed to actual sleep time; gradually increase as sleep improves.
    

### 4. Cultivate Productive Morning Habits

- Begin with basic needs, light meditation, and gratitude journaling.
    
- Perform light exercise.
    
- Avoid early phone use and instant gratification activities.
    

### 5. General Routine Principles

- Action and consistency are crucial for habit formation.
    
- Leverage the Compound Effect: small consistent actions yield big results.
    

### Time Tracking as a System

- Block recurring time for key activities.
    
- Assign categories to time slots for greater awareness.
    
- Log and review time to recognize and optimize behavioral archetypes.
    
- Time tracking increases clarity, flow, and productivity.
