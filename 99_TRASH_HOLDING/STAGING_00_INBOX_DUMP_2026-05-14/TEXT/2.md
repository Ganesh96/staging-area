### III. Failure, Mistakes, and Growth Mindset

| Previous Question | Tailored Advice (Integrating your input) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Tell me about a time you made a mistake that affected a colleague or the project. How did you rectify things?** | **(Similar to Q3: Couldn’t meet deadline)**: Use **STAR.** Be honest about how things didn't go as expected. The **Action** is key—focus on how you immediately owned the mistake, communicated clearly with all stakeholders, and put in place specific steps to rectify the situation. The **Result** must emphasize the reflective lesson learned and how you would handle it differently now. |
| **Tell me about a time you failed at work. What did you learn from that experience?** | **(Maps to Q3 & Q9: Weaknesses)**: Same advice as above. The emphasis here is squarely on the **Result/Reflection**—how did this failure lead to a demonstrable change in your approach, process, or technical skill (Growth Mindset). |
| **What would you say are your weaknesses and how would you improve them?** | **(Maps to Q9: Weaknesses)**: Be honest, choose a genuine, non-critical weakness for the SDE role (e.g., not public speaking, or optimizing prematurely). Clearly state the weakness, and then follow the advice: **Show them the action steps you have in place to improve it** (e.g., specific courses, shadowing, setting practice goals). |
| **What is the most constructive feedback you have received in your career?** | **(Maps to Q9: Weaknesses/Improvement)**: Frame the feedback as a **Situation**, your recognition as the **Task**, and the **Action** steps you took to improve it. This is a direct measure of your Growth Mindset. |
