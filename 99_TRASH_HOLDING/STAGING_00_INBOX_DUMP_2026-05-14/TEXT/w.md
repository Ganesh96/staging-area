1. Productivity & Time Management Strategies
This section outlines practical methods to structure your day, manage tasks, and optimize your work efficiency, drawing heavily from "Improve Habit- Productivity Beat Procrastination.pdf" and "productivity - texts.pdf".
•
Understand Key Frameworks
◦
Procrastination Framework: Break down large, intimidating tasks into smaller, manageable parts. For example, if writing a report, start by outlining sections and then focus on one section at a time. Create accountability by assigning rewards for completion or penalties for delays. This framework provides initial momentum.
◦
Time Blocking: Divide your day into dedicated blocks for specific tasks or categories (e.g., deep work, meetings, emails). Schedule both focused work and routine tasks. For instance, block 9 AM–11 AM for deep work and 11 AM–12 PM for meetings. This helps optimize task execution. The provided daily schedule in "Fixing Myself.pdf" (8-9 AM: Wake up and bathe; 9-10 AM: Talk to Appa and plan studies; 10 AM-12 PM: Leetcode and YouTube; 4-6 PM: Databases and SQL; 8-9 PM: Azure services basics; 9-10 PM: OOPS, Threading, Locks, Syncs) is an example of applying time blocking.
◦
Pomodoro Technique: Work in focused 25-minute intervals (one Pomodoro), followed by a 5-minute break. After four Pomodoros, take a longer break (15-30 minutes). This technique helps maintain focus and consistency.
◦
Eisenhower Matrix: Categorize your tasks based on urgency and importance into four quadrants:
▪
Urgent & Important: Do it first.
▪
Important, Not Urgent: Schedule it for later.
▪
Urgent, Not Important: Delegate if possible.
▪
Not Urgent & Not Important: Eliminate or postpone. This framework helps prioritize effectively.
◦
2-Minute Rule: If a task takes less than two minutes, do it immediately to prevent procrastination and clear mental clutter. Use the first 10-15 minutes of your day to reply to quick emails or organize your workspace. This builds momentum for larger tasks.
◦
Task Batching: Group similar tasks together to avoid task-switching. For instance, dedicate specific blocks in the morning and afternoon for emails and administrative work.
◦
Kanban Board: Set up a visual board with columns like “To Do,” “In Progress,” and “Done.” Move tasks across the board as you complete them to visualize progress.
•
Applying Strategies Throughout Your Day
◦
Morning Preparation: Use your peak energy hours for deep work, starting with a morning cue (e.g., coffee) followed by focused work in Pomodoro cycles. You can prioritize tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix at the start of your day.
◦
First Hour: Begin by clearing quick tasks using the 2-Minute Rule to build momentum.
◦
Mid-Morning: Block 2-3 hours for high-priority tasks and break them into smaller steps using the Procrastination Framework.
◦
Afternoon: Use Pomodoro intervals for secondary tasks and prioritize using the Eisenhower Matrix.
◦
Workday Structure: Incorporate Time Blocking, Pomodoro, and the 2-Minute Rule into your main work hours.
◦
End of Day: Spend 10-15 minutes reviewing accomplishments, updating your to-do list, and setting priorities for the next day.

2. Habit Formation & Personal Discipline
This section focuses on building and maintaining positive habits, crucial for long-term success and productivity.
•
Core Habit Principles
◦
Habit Formation Framework: Utilize the cue-routine-reward cycle to build new habits. For example, a cue (waking up) leads to a routine (30-minute workout), followed by a reward (a healthy breakfast). Set specific goals and create support systems to reinforce positive behaviors. This sustains productivity over the long term.
◦
Seinfeld Strategy ("Don't Break the Chain"): Mark each day you complete your habit on a calendar to create a visual streak. Aim to "not break the chain" to build consistency. The advice "never miss two days" can also help build consistency.
◦
Find Your Peak Time: Identify when you're most focused and energetic throughout the day. Schedule your most important tasks during this peak time to maximize productivity. For example, if you're most productive in the morning, allocate that time for high-priority work. This integrates well with Time Blocking for optimal scheduling.
◦
"Snowballing" Habits: Kangiten Uchiha "snowballs all the habits, discipline and creative work". This suggests building on existing small habits to create a larger impact over time.
•
Practical Application for Habits
◦
Consistency is Key: Incorporate small daily habits that align with your goals, like reading for 15 minutes or exercising.
◦
Start Small: Don't try to implement everything at once. Start with two or three techniques that resonate most, then add more as they become habits.
◦
Use Reminders: Set alarms on your phone to help stick to your routines.
◦
Address Negative Habits: Identify areas for improvement, including negative habits, their triggers, and potential substitutes. For procrastination, resources like Rian Doris's video are suggested.
◦
Personal Discipline: The ideal persona, Kangiten Uchiha, embodies many disciplined habits, such as waking early, meditating, physical training, maintaining hygiene, managing money, planning time, and controlling anger.

3. Goal Setting & Self-Improvement
This section focuses on defining your aspirations and cultivating the mindset and actions needed to achieve them.
•
Define Your Goals
◦
S.M.A.R.T. Goals: Set specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound goals. Each evening, spend 5-10 minutes setting one or two S.M.A.R.T. goals for the next day, such as "do a 30-minute cardio workout at 7 AM" instead of just "exercise".
◦
Areas for Improvement: Actively identify areas you want to improve, such as having a productive day/week, setting goals, addressing character flaws, and changing negative habits.
◦
"Draft what you want to be": A note to oneself suggests the importance of visualizing and defining your desired self.
•
Cultivate a Deserving Mindset
◦
Effort and Deserving Success: "Winning is a by-product for people who pay using their efforts". To become successful, you have to become someone who deserves it; "Life doesn’t owe you anything other than opportunities".
◦
Honest Work: Only people who keep doing honest work are the ones who board the "bus carrying all of the opportunities".
◦
Control What You Can: Figure out the controllable and uncontrollable aspects of any situation. Focus on tasks and routines to control the controllable.
◦
Positive Mindset: Actively work on staying positive. When feeling "stupid," focus on putting in "more efforts." When "tired," push through for "5 more minutes".
◦
Embrace the Journey: Don't pay attention to what others think of you. Focus on what interests you most.
•
Aspirational Traits (from Kangiten Uchiha persona)
◦
Goal-Oriented: Be goal-oriented and know how to plan for the short (3 months) and long term (6 months, 1 year).
◦
Holistic Development: Strive to improve technical, financial, and business acumen. Manage money well, understand investment and debt management, and develop entrepreneurship skills.
◦
Personal Growth: Develop a healthy ego, remember and honor promises, treat everyone with respect, control anger, and be a deep thinker who can focus for hours.

4. Learning & Skill Development
This section compiles advice on continuous learning, skill acquisition, and leveraging educational resources.
•
Foundational Learning Principles
◦
Continuous Learning: "Never stop learning". This is a core aspect of personal development.
◦
Read and Study Daily: "Read everyday" and "Study hard" are fundamental recommendations.
◦
Ask Questions and Embrace Not Knowing: "Ask questions" and remember "It's Okay to not know things!". This fosters an open mind for learning.
◦
Learn from Mistakes: "Make mistakes and learn". This emphasizes an iterative approach to skill development.
◦
Teach Others: "Teach others what you know". Teaching can solidify your own understanding.
•
Learning Preferences and Motivation
◦
Internal Motivation: A strong drive comes from the capability of computer science, mathematics, and science to improve individual abilities to invent, design, or solve problems. There's an affinity for problem-solving and automation.
◦
Extrinsic Motivation: Motivation also comes from upgrading lifestyle, status, and providing for dependents.
◦
Structured Learning: A preference for structured learning provides a concrete path and motivation due to the ability to measure progress.
◦
Audiovisual Preference: While reading is an underrated skill, audiovisual learning is often preferred for absorbing concepts effectively.
◦
Learning by Doing: Due to the domain, there's an advantage in practicing by doing to rectify and improvise on mistakes.
◦
Leverage Prior Knowledge: Strong mathematical background is beneficial for solving complex problems and modifying mathematical concepts due to their logical solutions.
◦
Challenging Problems: These require improving your understanding of familiar concepts.
◦
Easy Tasks: Coming up with solutions for CS problems/products is easy due to familiarity with product development, logical thinking, design thinking, and a strong computer science background.
•
Recommended Learning Resources & Focus Areas

Online Learning Platforms: Utilize Pluralsight and LinkedIn Learning for skill-building. Coursera (e.g., Algorithms courses, Containers) and Neetcode (DSA, System Design) are also referenced.

Flash Cards: Incorporate a flash card routine for skill-building.

Specific Technical Areas: Focus on Leetcode (especially Leetcode 75) for coding interviews. Study Databases and SQL, Azure services basics, OOPS, Threading, Locks, Syncs. Prepare for System Design interviews using blogs and study groups. Familiarize yourself with design patterns like Command.
◦
"Devour Information": Kangiten Uchiha "devours information to improve technical, financial and business acumen". This highlights a broad, continuous learning approach.
◦
Information Retention: Learn how to retain lots of information and develop a "great second brain system" to manage tasks, projects, and resources.

5. Career & Job Search Routines
This section outlines systematic approaches for job searching and interview preparation, drawing primarily from "Weekly.pdf" and "_notes.pdf".
•
Weekly Job Search Activities
◦
Company Research: Jot down companies of interest and group them.
◦
Alerts and Checking: Set alerts and check company portals daily or every two days for new job postings.
◦
Portfolio Management: Maintain and update your Github portfolio.
◦
Interview Preparation: Dedicate time to prepare for all interview types: Coding Interview, Behavioral Interview, System Design Interview, and Final Interview.
•
Daily Job Search Activities
◦
Morning (8 AM - 12 PM):
▪
Apply for previous day's job postings.
▪
Write cover letters on the spot for applications.
▪
Practice Leetcode.
◦
Evening (3 PM - 5 PM):
▪
Accumulate 2-3 new job postings.
▪
Fine-tune your resume for specific job postings.
▪
Reach out to existing connections for referrals.
▪
Set up calls with people in target organizations (e.g., via Calendly).
▪
Find and add new connections on LinkedIn, especially technical recruiters and senior individuals in your area of interest.
•
Specific Preparation Focus Areas
◦
System Design: Dedicate Fridays to System Design study using resources like MiLlan, Quastor, Ashish Pratap Singh, and system design blogs. Links to system design courses (e.g., Neetcode) are also provided.
◦
Behavioral Questions: Prepare for behavioral questions, specifically STAR questions related to your resume, strengths, weaknesses, challenges, and critical situations.
◦
Interview Resources: Utilize resources like interviewing.io for mock interviews, guides on Amazon SDE loops, and salary negotiation tips.
◦
Resume/LinkedIn Optimization: Ensure your LinkedIn profile adds details for experiences, uses bite-sized paragraphs, and highlights daily activities, impacts, and achievements.

6. Overall Mindset & Daily Reflection
This section consolidates advice on cultivating a resilient mindset, managing emotions, and engaging in regular self-assessment.
•
Mindset and Self-Perception
◦
Positive Outlook: Strive to "Stay positive". Acknowledge when you feel "stupid" and respond with "more efforts". When tired, push through for "5 more minutes".
◦
Embrace Mistakes and Learning: "Make mistakes and learn" and remember "It's Okay to not know things!".
◦
Focus on Your Path: "Don't pay attention to what others think of you".
◦
Control What You Can: Distinguish between controllable and uncontrollable factors in situations and focus your energy on what you can influence.
◦
Emotional Intelligence: Learn how to control anger.
◦
Authenticity: Engage in hobbies and interests you genuinely enjoy, such as strategic games like football and shogi, and learning new technical concepts.
•
Reflection and Review Practices
◦
Productivity Journaling: At the end of each day, jot down what you achieved, any challenges, and ideas for improvement. Include thoughts on how you felt during peak productivity times. This helps in continuous self-assessment.
◦
Closing Out the Day: Spend the last 10-15 minutes of your day reviewing accomplishments, updating your to-do list, and setting priorities for the next day.
◦
Weekly Review: At the end of each week, review your productivity journal, goals, and tasks. Assess where you excelled and where improvements can be made, then adjust your strategies accordingly.
◦
Decision-Making Frameworks: Use structured tools like the 5 Whys to analyze why tasks might be delayed or challenging, and the Cynefin Framework to adjust strategies.
◦
Ethical Reflection: Use the THINK Method (Truthful, Helpful, Inspiring, Necessary, Kind) to reflect on your actions.
•
Holistic Well-being
◦
Physical Health: "Wake up early and stay healthy diet". Kangiten Uchiha also "meditates and physically trains" and "has healthy habits that makes him better at sports, career and in life".
◦
Nature and Rest: "Spend time with nature". Ensure you schedule time for rest and eating.
◦
Minimalism: Own minimal materials, based only on utility.
◦
Hygiene: Maintain good hygiene, both personal and environmental.
◦
Communication: Speak clearly and concisely. Know how to convince people and negotiate.
