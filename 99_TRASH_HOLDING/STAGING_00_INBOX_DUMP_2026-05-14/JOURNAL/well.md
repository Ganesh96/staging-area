B. Procrastination and Discipline:
•
Procrastination Framework: Break large, intimidating tasks into smaller, manageable parts. Create accountability by assigning rewards for completion or penalties for delays.
•
2-Minute Rule: If a task takes less than two minutes, do it immediately to prevent procrastination and build momentum.
•
Confront Escape Triggers: Identify specific moments when distractions are sought and create "if-then" plans (e.g., if feeling the urge to scroll, do 5 push-ups) to rewire behavior with action, not avoidance.
•
Utilize resources like Rian Doris's video on procrastination.
C. Time Management and Productivity:
•
Establish a Routine: Use tools like Habitica to create daily tasks and habits aligned with goals, setting reminders and rewards.
•
Time Blocking: Divide the day into dedicated blocks for specific tasks (e.g., deep work, meetings, emails). Schedule both focused work and routine tasks.
•
Pomodoro Technique: Work in focused 25-minute intervals (Pomodoros) followed by 5-minute breaks, taking longer breaks after four Pomodoros.
•
Eisenhower Matrix: Categorize tasks based on urgency and importance: "Urgent & Important (Do first)," "Important, Not Urgent (Schedule)," "Urgent, Not Important (Delegate)," and "Not Urgent & Not Important (Eliminate/Postpone)".
•
Find Your Peak Time: Identify periods of highest focus and energy to schedule the most important tasks.
•
S.M.A.R.T. Goals: Set specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound goals daily.
•
Task Batching: Group similar tasks (e.g., emails) to avoid task-switching.
•
Kanban Board: Utilize columns like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done" to visualize and manage task flow.
•
Closing Out the Day: Spend 10-15 minutes reviewing accomplishments, updating to-do lists, and setting priorities for the next day.
•
Productivity Journaling: Daily reflection on achievements, challenges, and ideas for improvement.
•
Weekly Review: Regularly assess progress, identify areas for improvement, and adjust strategies.
•
Realistic Minimum Viable Effort (MVE): Focus on one small, consistent daily effort (e.g., 15 minutes of coding) to build momentum and overcome overwhelm.
