## Part 3: How to Avoid Craving and Procrastination

### 1. Understand Dopamine and Reward Sensitivity

- Dopamine drives motivation, learning, and reward.
    
- Overstimulation causes desensitization, making low-dopamine activities feel dull.
    
- Long-term habit change is key to resensitization—not short dopamine "detoxes".
    

### 2. Strategies to Resensitize Dopamine and Boost Focus

- Take boring breaks: engage in non-stimulating recovery.
    
- Avoid high-dopamine distractions during breaks.
    
- Use transitional moments to be present.
    
- Practice singular focus: one task at a time for full attention.
    

### 3. Combat Procrastination by Addressing Fear

- Procrastination is often a stress-relief strategy rooted in fear.
    
- Fear of failure, judgment, or inadequacy activates avoidance behavior.
    

### 4. Micro-Experiments to Overcome Procrastination and Fear

- 60-Second Rule: Acknowledge fear, countdown from 5, get started for 2 minutes.
    
- Label emotions and fears aloud to reduce their intensity.
    
- Identity Labels: Reframe self-perception with empowering identities.
    
- 10-10-10 Rule: Reassess the long-term impact of setbacks.
    
- Confidence = perceived ability – perceived standard. Lower the bar to get started.
    
- Diminish the spotlight effect: others care less than we think.
    
- Batman Effect: Act through an alter ego to access inner strength.
    

### 5. Leverage Purpose and Goals

- Lack of motivation is often rooted in a lack of purpose.
    
- Create a hierarchy of goals from long-term vision to daily tasks.
    
- Focus on one meaningful project aligned with your biggest challenge.
    
- Schedule lever-moving tasks during high-energy hours.
    
- Daily progress on meaningful goals releases healthy dopamine and lowers distraction.
