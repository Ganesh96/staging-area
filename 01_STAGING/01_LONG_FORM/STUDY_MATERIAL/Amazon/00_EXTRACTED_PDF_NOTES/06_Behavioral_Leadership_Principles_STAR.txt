# Behavioral Interview, Amazon Leadership Principles, and STAR Story System

Core expectation
Amazon behavioral interviews evaluate how you have demonstrated Leadership Principles in real professional situations. Interviewers want specific examples, individual ownership, data, tradeoffs, and learning.

Primary answer format: STAR

Situation
Describe the context:
- where it occurred
- when it happened
- background
- relevant constraints
- what was at stake

Task
Describe your responsibility:
- your role
- goal
- why it mattered
- risks or consequences if nothing changed

Action
Describe what you personally did:
- specific steps
- decisions made
- influence used
- obstacles faced
- tradeoffs
- mechanisms created
- how you handled conflict or ambiguity

Result
Describe outcome:
- metrics
- customer/business impact
- quality improvement
- lessons learned
- what you would do differently
- long-term mechanism or prevention

Critical rule
Use "I", not "we", when describing actions. Team context is allowed, but the interviewer is evaluating your contribution.

STAR quality bar
A strong STAR answer has:
- specific event, not generic pattern
- clear stakes
- personal ownership
- concrete actions
- obstacles
- tradeoffs
- measurable result
- learning/reflection
- Leadership Principle alignment

Weak answer patterns:
- "We worked on..."
- no metrics
- no obstacle
- no decision
- no tradeoff
- no failure or tension
- too scripted
- not answering question asked
- vague claims like "I communicated well"

Build a story bank

Step 1 - List significant professional moments
Create 10-15 moments from recent roles:
- goals achieved
- risks taken
- failures
- conflict
- ambiguity
- technical depth
- customer impact
- operational incident
- mentorship
- quality improvement
- process improvement
- deadline pressure
- wrong decision corrected
- disagreement
- invention/simplification
- cost reduction

Step 2 - Fill STAR details
For each moment:
Situation:
- context/background
- where/when
- goal

Task:
- your role
- what you were trying to achieve
- why important
- risks if nothing happened

Action:
- what you personally owned
- how you did it
- how you influenced outcome
- who else was involved
- biggest obstacle

Result:
- how success was measured
- results and metrics
- tradeoffs made
- learning or what you would do differently

Step 3 - Map to Leadership Principles
Each story can map to multiple principles. Use this to avoid needing 16 separate stories.

Step 4 - Prepare follow-up details
Interviewers will dive deep.
Prepare:
- exact metrics
- timeline
- alternatives
- why you chose the approach
- what failed
- how you handled pushback
- what you learned
- what mechanism prevented recurrence

Leadership Principles and interview interpretation

1. Customer Obsession
Definition:
Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They earn and keep customer trust. They pay attention to competitors but obsess over customers.

What interviewers look for:
- understanding customer pain
- prioritizing customer impact
- making tradeoffs for customer trust
- recovering from missed expectations
- measuring customer outcomes

Story types:
- improved customer experience
- fixed customer-impacting defect
- prioritized customer needs under constraints
- apologized/recovered from failure
- changed roadmap due to customer signal

Prompts:
- Who was your most difficult customer?
- Tell me about a time you did not meet a client/customer expectation.
- How do you prioritize needs across many customers?
- Tell the story of the last time you had to apologize to someone.

2. Ownership
Definition:
Leaders think long term, act on behalf of the entire company, and never say "that is not my job."

What interviewers look for:
- taking responsibility beyond assigned scope
- long-term thinking
- accepting outcomes
- persistence
- initiative
- business/customer judgment

Story types:
- fixed issue outside your direct ownership
- owned ambiguous project
- took responsibility for failure
- improved system/process long term
- prevented recurring incidents

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you had to leave a task unfinished.
- Tell me about a time you worked on a project with unclear responsibilities.
- Give an example of a risk you took that failed.

3. Invent and Simplify
Definition:
Leaders innovate and simplify. They seek ideas from everywhere and are not limited by "not invented here."

What interviewers look for:
- simplification of complex systems/processes
- invention under constraints
- external awareness
- practical innovation
- reducing operational burden

Story types:
- simplified architecture
- automated manual process
- created new tool
- reduced complexity
- improved developer/customer workflow

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you gave a simple solution to a complex problem.
- Tell me about a time you created a new way of doing something that gave a competitive advantage.
- Tell me about an out-of-the-box idea and its impact.

4. Are Right, A Lot
Definition:
Leaders have strong judgment and seek diverse perspectives. They work to disconfirm beliefs.

What interviewers look for:
- sound judgment under uncertainty
- data-informed decisions
- willingness to challenge own assumptions
- learning from being wrong
- seeking perspectives

Story types:
- made decision with incomplete data
- changed mind after evidence
- predicted risk others missed
- corrected wrong assumption
- used metrics + anecdote

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you were wrong.
- Tell me about a time you worked with incomplete data.
- Tell me about a solution others doubted that turned out right.

5. Learn and Be Curious
Definition:
Leaders keep learning and explore new possibilities.

What interviewers look for:
- self-driven learning
- feedback seeking
- skill development
- curiosity leading to impact
- adapting quickly

Story types:
- learned new tech to solve problem
- sought feedback and improved
- asked questions that changed direction
- explored new approach
- developed skill outside required scope

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you influenced change by only asking questions.
- Tell me about a time you solved a problem through superior knowledge or observation.

6. Hire and Develop the Best
Definition:
Leaders raise the performance bar, recognize talent, develop leaders, and coach others.

What interviewers look for:
- mentorship
- raising standards
- coaching
- feedback
- helping others grow
- dealing with poor performance constructively

Story types:
- mentored junior engineer
- improved onboarding
- gave difficult feedback
- coached teammate through issue
- created development mechanism

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you mentored someone.
- Tell me about a time you dealt with a poor performer.

7. Insist on the Highest Standards
Definition:
Leaders continually raise the bar and ensure defects are fixed so they stay fixed.

What interviewers look for:
- quality ownership
- refusing shortcuts
- mechanisms for prevention
- technical excellence
- constructive behavior under pressure

Story types:
- improved code quality
- prevented defect from shipping
- built test automation
- raised review standards
- fixed root cause instead of symptom
- refused to compromise standards

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you could not meet your own expectations.
- Tell me about a time a team member did not meet your expectations.
- Tell me about a time you refused to compromise code quality.

8. Think Big
Definition:
Leaders create and communicate bold direction and look around corners for ways to serve customers.

What interviewers look for:
- vision beyond immediate task
- scaling impact
- customer-focused future thinking
- bold but practical direction
- communicating roadmap

Story types:
- expanded scope for larger impact
- proposed long-term architecture
- identified future customer need
- turned local fix into platform improvement

Prompts:
- Tell me about your proudest professional achievement.
- Tell me about a time you went beyond project scope and delivered.

9. Bias for Action
Definition:
Speed matters. Many decisions are reversible and do not need extensive study. Calculated risk is valued.

What interviewers look for:
- acting quickly with enough information
- identifying reversible decisions
- calculated risk
- unblocking others
- urgency without recklessness

Story types:
- acted before perfect data
- fixed urgent problem
- made reversible decision quickly
- drove stalled project
- got information from unresponsive stakeholder

Prompts:
- Describe a time you saw a problem and took initiative.
- Tell me about a calculated risk.
- Tell me about getting information from someone unresponsive.
- Tell me about making a decision without time to weigh all options.

10. Frugality
Definition:
Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness.

What interviewers look for:
- resource constraints
- cost awareness
- clever simplification
- avoiding unnecessary headcount/budget
- ROI thinking

Story types:
- reduced cloud cost
- optimized resource usage
- delivered without extra headcount
- reused existing system
- automated manual work

Prompts:
- Tell me about working with limited time/resources.
- Tell me about needing a bigger budget but not getting it.
- Tell me about saving money for the company.

11. Earn Trust
Definition:
Leaders listen, speak candidly, treat others respectfully, are vocally self-critical, and benchmark against the best.

What interviewers look for:
- candid communication
- humility
- respect
- trust repair
- self-criticism
- conflict handling

Story types:
- admitted mistake
- delivered harsh truth respectfully
- rebuilt trust
- handled stakeholder conflict
- sought feedback

Prompts:
- What would you do if your closest friend at work was stealing?
- Tell me about a time you told someone a harsh truth.
- Tell me about a time you earned trust of a group.

12. Dive Deep
Definition:
Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ.

What interviewers look for:
- technical depth
- root cause analysis
- data investigation
- debugging
- auditing
- connecting metrics and details

Story types:
- investigated production issue
- found root cause in data
- discovered metric discrepancy
- debugged hard technical issue
- improved observability

Prompts:
- Tell me about a time you devised a new way of looking at data.
- Describe personally resolving a challenging technical situation.
- Tell me about diving deep into data and results achieved.

13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
Definition:
Leaders respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, then commit once a decision is made.

What interviewers look for:
- respectful disagreement
- conviction
- evidence-based challenge
- courage
- commitment after decision

Story types:
- challenged technical approach
- disagreed with manager
- pushed back on timeline/quality
- committed after team decision
- raised risk others ignored

Prompts:
- Tell me about an unpopular decision.
- Tell me about stepping up and disagreeing with a teammate's approach.
- If your manager instructed something you disagreed with, how would you handle it?

14. Deliver Results
Definition:
Leaders focus on key inputs and deliver quality results on time despite setbacks.

What interviewers look for:
- execution
- prioritization
- resilience
- outcome focus
- timely delivery
- quality under pressure

Story types:
- delivered under tight deadline
- handled multiple assignments
- overcame obstacle
- pivoted strategy late in project
- recovered failing project

Prompts:
- Tell me about handling a variety of assignments.
- Give an example of pivoting 75% through a project and succeeding.
- Tell me about overcoming an obstacle and delivering results.

15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
Definition:
Leaders create safer, more productive, diverse, higher-performing, more just work environments and support employee growth.

What interviewers look for:
- empathy
- team health
- inclusion
- empowering others
- growth mindset
- psychological safety

Story types:
- improved team process
- supported teammate growth
- created safer feedback loop
- improved onboarding
- reduced burnout

16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
Definition:
Leaders think about secondary effects of actions, act humbly, and leave things better.

What interviewers look for:
- second-order thinking
- responsible scaling
- community/customer/employee impact
- long-term consequences
- humility about imperfect systems

Story types:
- identified unintended consequences
- improved privacy/security/reliability
- reduced negative externality
- made scalable process more responsible

Behavioral answer template
"Situation: [specific context]. Task: I was responsible for [goal/stakes]. Action: I did [specific actions], including [tradeoff/conflict/decision]. Result: This led to [metric/outcome]. I learned [lesson], and if doing it again I would [improvement]."

Story bank matrix

For each story, fill:
- Title
- Date / role
- Situation
- Task
- Actions
- Results
- Metrics
- Main LPs
- Backup LPs
- Failure/tension
- Tradeoff
- Follow-up details
- What I would do differently

Recommended 10-15 story categories
1. Customer-impacting incident
2. Ambiguous project
3. Technical deep dive/root cause
4. Code quality/standards
5. Fast decision with incomplete data
6. Disagreement with teammate/manager
7. Mentorship/coaching
8. Cost/resource constraint
9. Simplification/invention
10. Project delivered under deadline
11. Failure and recovery
12. Data-driven decision
13. Scaling/reliability improvement
14. Cross-team influence
15. Personal learning/growth

Behavioral interview checklist
Before answering:
- identify which LP is being tested
- choose best matching story
- answer exact question
- keep story specific
- quantify result
- use "I"
- include tradeoff/obstacle
- prepare for follow-up

After answering:
- pause
- let interviewer ask for depth
- do not keep adding unrelated details
