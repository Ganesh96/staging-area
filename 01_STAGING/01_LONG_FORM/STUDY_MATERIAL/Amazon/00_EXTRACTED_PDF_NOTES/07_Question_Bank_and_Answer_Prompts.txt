# Question Bank and Answer-Prep Prompts

Purpose
This file consolidates question prompts from the PDFs and organizes them for practice. Use these to build and test your story bank.

Behavioral questions by Leadership Principle

Customer Obsession
- Who was your most difficult customer?
- Give me an example of a time when you did not meet a client's expectation. What happened, and how did you rectify it?
- When working with many customers, how do you prioritize customer needs?
- Tell the story of the last time you had to apologize to someone.
- Tell me about a time you improved customer trust.
- Tell me about a time customer feedback changed your approach.

Ownership
- Tell me about a time when you had to leave a task unfinished.
- Tell me about a time when you worked on a project with unclear responsibilities.
- Give me an example of when you took a risk and it failed.
- Tell me about a time you took responsibility for something outside your role.
- Tell me about a time you fixed a long-term issue rather than a short-term symptom.

Invent and Simplify
- Tell me about a time when you gave a simple solution to a complex problem.
- Tell me about a time when you created a new way of doing something that gave the company a competitive advantage.
- Tell me about an out-of-the-box idea you had and its impact.
- Tell me about a time you automated or simplified a manual process.
- Tell me about a time your idea was misunderstood at first.

Are Right, A Lot
- Tell me about a time when you were wrong.
- Tell me about a time when you worked with incomplete data or information.
- Tell me about a time when you proposed a solution others doubted that turned out in your favor.
- Tell me about a time you changed your mind after new evidence.
- Tell me about a time you sought diverse perspectives before deciding.

Insist on the Highest Standards
- Tell me about a time when you could not meet your own expectations on a project.
- Tell me about a time when a team member did not meet your expectations.
- Tell me about a time you refused to compromise your standards around code quality.
- Tell me about a time you prevented a defect from being shipped.
- Tell me about a time you raised the quality bar for a team.

Learn and Be Curious
- Tell me about a time when you influenced change by only asking questions.
- Tell me about a time when you solved a problem through superior knowledge or observation.
- Tell me about a time you learned a new technology to solve a problem.
- Tell me about a time feedback changed your behavior.
- Tell me about a time you explored a new possibility and acted on it.

Think Big
- Tell me about your proudest professional achievement.
- Tell me about a time when you went way beyond the project scope and delivered.
- Tell me about a time you looked around corners for a future customer need.
- Tell me about a time you proposed a larger vision than the original ask.

Bias for Action
- Describe a time when you saw a problem and took initiative instead of waiting.
- Tell me about a time when you took a calculated risk.
- Tell me about a time you needed information from someone who was not responsive. What did you do?
- Tell me about a time you did not have time to weigh all options but had to decide.
- Tell me about a time you acted with incomplete data but used judgment.

Frugality
- Tell me about a time when you had to work with limited time or resources.
- Describe a time when you needed a bigger budget to achieve desired results but were not granted it. How did you overcome the challenge?
- Tell me about a clever new way you saved money for the company.
- Tell me about a time you achieved more with less.
- Tell me about a time constraints led to invention.

Earn Trust
- What would you do if you found out your closest friend at work was stealing?
- Tell me about a time when you had to tell someone a harsh truth.
- Tell me about a time when you earned trust of a group.
- Tell me about a time you admitted a mistake.
- Tell me about a time you repaired trust after a failure.

Dive Deep
- Tell me about a time when you devised a new way of looking at data that improved performance.
- Describe a time when you personally resolved a challenging technical situation.
- Tell me about a time when you had to dive deep into data and the results you achieved.
- Tell me about a time metrics and anecdotes disagreed.
- Tell me about a root cause analysis you led.

Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
- Tell me about an unpopular decision of yours.
- Tell me about a time when you stepped up and disagreed with a team member's approach.
- If your direct manager instructed you to do something you disagreed with, how would you handle it?
- Tell me about a time you challenged "the way it has always been done."
- Tell me about a time you committed after your preferred approach was not chosen.

Hire and Develop the Best
- Tell me about a time when you mentored someone.
- Tell me about a time when you dealt with a poor performer on your team.
- Tell me about a time you helped someone grow.
- Tell me about a mechanism you created for team development.
- Tell me about a time you raised the performance bar.

Deliver Results
- Tell me about a time you handled a variety of assignments. What were the results?
- Give an example of a time when you were 75% through a project and had to pivot strategy. How did you make it a success?
- Tell me about a time when you overcame an obstacle and delivered results.
- Give an example of delivering an important project under a tight deadline.
- Tell me about a project that did not go as planned and how you would improve next time.

Business-critical and independent decision prompts
- Think about times you made business-critical decisions independently.
- Tell me about a decision you made without all the data you wanted.
- Tell me about a time your intuition shaped direction.
- Tell me about a time you balanced speed and correctness.
- Tell me about a time you made a tradeoff that affected customers.

Follow-up questions interviewers may ask
- What exactly was your role?
- What did you personally do?
- What alternatives did you consider?
- Why did you choose that approach?
- What data did you have?
- What data did you wish you had?
- What was the hardest part?
- Who disagreed and why?
- How did you influence them?
- What was the outcome?
- How did you measure success?
- What would you do differently?
- What did you learn?
- Did the mechanism continue after you left?
- How did this affect customers?
- How did this affect the business?
- What was the long-term impact?

Technical coding prompts

General coding questions
- Solve one coding problem in the language of your choice.
- Write production-like code.
- Explain what you are trying to achieve as you code.
- Add test cases.
- Brainstorm edge cases.
- State time and space complexity.
- Explain tradeoffs and improvements.

- Valid Parentheses
- Two Sum
- Longest Common Prefix
- Merge Sorted Array
- Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock
- Maximum Depth of Binary Tree

Arrays

- Longest Substring Without Repeating Characters
- Search in Rotated Sorted Array
- Remove Duplicates from Sorted Array
- Container With Most Water
- Valid Sudoku
- Top K Frequent Elements

Strings
- Reverse String
- Length of Last Word
- Valid Anagram
- Decode String
- String Compression
- Unique Email Addresses
- Isomorphic Strings

Linked Lists
- Reverse Linked List
- Remove Nth Node From End of List
- Swap Nodes in Pairs
- Add Two Numbers
- Palindrome Linked List
- Copy Linked List With Random Pointer
- Linked List Cycle
- Odd-Even Linked List

Trees and traversals
- Binary Tree Level Order Traversal
- Diameter of Binary Tree
- Balanced Binary Tree
- Symmetric Tree
- Path Sum
- Delete Node in a BST
- Validate Binary Search Tree
- Max Area of Island
- Binary Tree Right Side View

Dynamic Programming
- House Robber
- Climbing Stairs

- Longest Increasing Subsequence

Miscellaneous

- Find Median from Data Stream
- Course Schedule
- Network Delay Time

System design prompts

System design general prompts
- Design a software system.
- Design a scalable service.
- Reverse-engineer your favorite app.
- Design Twitter.
- Design TinyURL.
- Design a parking lot system.
- Design Uber.
- Design Instagram.
- Design a Tinder-like service.
- Design an image upload/retrieval service.

System design clarifying questions
- Who is the customer?
- What problem are we solving?
- What are the core use cases?
- What are functional requirements?
- What are non-functional requirements?
- What scale should we support?
- What is the read/write ratio?
- What latency do users expect?
- What availability do we need?
- What consistency do we need?
- What are security/privacy requirements?

System design deep-dive prompts
- How will data be stored?
- What APIs are needed?
- What are the main components?
- What are the relationships between components?
- What are data flows?
- How will the system scale?
- Where will caching help?
- How will load balancing work?
- Should the database be relational or non-relational?
- How will sharding work?
- What happens if a component fails?
- What are possible points of failure?
- How can the design become more robust?
- How will you monitor the system?
- How will you troubleshoot performance problems?
- What are deployment considerations?
- What tradeoffs did you make?
- What would you change with more time?

Resume defense prompts
For each technology on your resume:
- Where did you use it?
- Why did you choose it?
- What alternatives did you consider?
- What scale did it support?
- What failure modes did you handle?
- What tradeoffs did it create?
- What would you improve?
- What did you personally implement?
- How did you test it?
- What metrics improved?

Why Amazon prompts
- Why Amazon?
- Why this role?
- What customer problem interests you?
- How would Amazon be mutually beneficial for you and the company?
- What part of Amazon's builder culture appeals to you?
- What technical scale or problem space excites you?

Questions to ask interviewer
- What customer problems is the team solving?
- What systems would this role own?
- What are the most important technical challenges?
- What does success look like in the first 6 months?
- How does the team measure operational health?
- What are the biggest scaling bottlenecks?
- How does the team balance new features with operational excellence?
- What are common traits of successful engineers on this team?
