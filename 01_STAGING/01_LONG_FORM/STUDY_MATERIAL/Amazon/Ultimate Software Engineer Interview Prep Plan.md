## **Beyond Coding: The Full Preparation Checklist**

Technical skills are only half the battle. Use this checklist to ensure you're ready for every part of the interview loop.

### **1. Behavioral Interview Preparation**

_Your past behavior is the best predictor of your future performance. Prepare concise, impactful stories._

- [ ] **Brainstorm Your Stories:** List 10-15 significant projects or situations from your career. Think about successes, failures, conflicts, and complex challenges.
    
- [ ] **Master the STAR Method:** For each story, structure it using **S**ituation (context), **T**ask (your responsibility), **A**ction (what _you_ specifically did), and **R**esult (the outcome, with metrics if possible).
    
- [ ] **Map Stories to Leadership Principles (Amazon Focus):**
    
    - [ ] Customer Obsession
        
    - [ ] Ownership
        
    - [ ] Invent and Simplify
        
    - [ ] Are Right, A Lot
        
    - [ ] Learn and Be Curious
        
    - [ ] Hire and Develop the Best
        
    - [ ] Insist on the Highest Standards
        
    - [ ] Think Big
        
    - [ ] Bias for Action
        
    - [ ] Frugality
        
    - [ ] Earn Trust
        
    - [ ] Dive Deep
        
    - [ ] Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
        
    - [ ] Deliver Results
        
    - [ ] Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
        
    - [ ] Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
        
- [ ] **Prepare for "Why this company?":** Research the company's mission, products, and recent challenges. Have a genuine and specific reason.
    
- [ ] **Prepare Questions for the Interviewer:** Prepare 3-5 thoughtful questions about the team, culture, technical challenges, or career growth.
    

### **2. System Design Review**

_Crucial for mid-to-senior level roles. Even for junior roles, understanding basic concepts is a plus._

- [ ] **Review Core Concepts:**
    
    - [ ] Vertical vs. Horizontal Scaling
        
    - [ ] Load Balancing (Round Robin, Least Connections, etc.)
        
    - [ ] Caching (Strategies: Cache-Aside, Write-Through; Eviction Policies: LRU, LFU)
        
    - [ ] Databases (SQL vs. NoSQL, CAP Theorem, Sharding, Replication)
        
    - [ ] API Design (REST, GraphQL)
        
    - [ ] Asynchronous Processing (Message Queues, Pub/Sub)
        
- [ ] **Study Common Architectures:**
    
    - [ ] Design a URL Shortener
        
    - [ ] Design a Social Media Feed (e.g., Twitter, Instagram)
        
    - [ ] Design a Ride-Sharing App (e.g., Uber)
        
    - [ ] Design a Chat Application (e.g., WhatsApp)
        
    - [ ] Design a Video Streaming Service (e.g., YouTube)
        

### **3. Mock Interviews & Final Polish**

_Practice is the only way to simulate real interview pressure._

- [ ] **Peer Mock Interviews:** Find a partner to practice with. Give and receive constructive feedback on both technical and communication skills.
    
- [ ] **Professional Mock Interviews:** Consider platforms like Pramp (free) or Interviewing.io (paid) for unbiased feedback from experienced engineers.
    
- [ ] **Timed LeetCode Sessions:** Solve problems under a time constraint (e.g., 45 minutes) to mimic a real interview pace. Practice verbalizing your solution as you code.
    
- [ ] **Resume Deep Dive:** Be ready to discuss every single point on your resume in detail. Why did you choose that technology? What was the impact? What would you do differently?
    

Good luck! Consistency is key.
